How to Prevent Food Adulteration?

Steps to Take Care While Transporting and Warehousing Food Products

Insect infestation due to the humid and unhygienic conditions of the warehouse is a huge menace for food grains, legumes, and spices. To resolve this problem, professional individuals should closely monitor the storage conditions and ensure that infested grains from fields do not reach the warehouse and become a threat to healthy grains. To improve the quality of grains, they should keep track of incoming and outgoing samples along with their dates. During the monsoon season and in highly humid areas, extra care must be taken to prevent spoilage of grains. Design a warehouse with a proper storage environment that minimizes the risk of contamination.

Steps to Take Care While Processing Food Products

To prevent the outbreaks of Salmonella, E. coli, and Campylobacter, certain food safety standards and regulations should be adhered to. Facility location should be designed to avoid pest "hot spots" and pollution areas. Machinery design should adhere to sanitizing and cleaning protocols. Invest in pest control monitoring and detection. Proper waste disposal should be a priority to avoid the risk of contamination. Personal and environmental hygiene should be benchmarked properly.

Steps to Take Care While Packaging Food Products

Packaging material should protect against chemical and microbial contamination. The ink used on packages should be free from hazardous substances. Proper labeling of food items, including allergen information, should be done to eradicate product recalls. Personal hygiene and good manufacturing facilities should be the major priority. Food packaging regulations should be properly implemented.

How to Prevent Food Adulteration?

On the industry level, food adulteration can only be checked with strict and stringent laws and government interventions and checks. The general public should avoid eating dark-colored, junk, and other processed foods. Washing fruits and vegetables thoroughly in running water before consumption is recommended. Check for leakages in canned foods and validate seals in milk or oil pouches. Always check and buy products with an FSSAI-validated label, license number, list of ingredients, manufacturing date, and expiration.

Conclusion

Protecting the health of the consumer along with rights must be the primary goal. Besides, preventing fraud or wrong practices are important and challenging issues facing the food industry. So, the food industry and manufacturers must take also perform their part to curb the menace of food adulteration. But, refusing to help by that adulterants are unknown, and difficultly to recognize using the targeted screening methods from the business communities cannot be accepted. Besides, these manufacturers and companies must also provide and confirm the authenticity and source of food products and their components. Besides, FSSAI proposal to enforce stringent law to combat the growing measures must be implemented.
